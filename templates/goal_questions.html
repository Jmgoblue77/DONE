How do I create a goal?

  1. Enter what your goal is in the first row

  2. Think of what the most likely obstacles will on the way to your goal. Determine what your responses to these obstacles will be. Enter these in the next rows.

  3. You do not need to fill out all of the obstacle response-pairs.

Example

  1. Eat healthy.

  2. Obstacle 1: seeing a soda

  3. Response 1: If I have not drunk a soda this week, I can have it. Otherwise, I will grab a water bottle

  4. Obstacle 2: seeing delicious but unhealthy options on a restaurant menu

  5. Response 2: I will order a salad.

Then what?

  • Then, just follow your plan!

  • You can see all your goals you have set under View in the upper navbar.

  • Under each goal, you will be given simple, if-then statements for what you will do if you come across the obstacle. Feel free to tweak the grammar if necessary.

Why does this work?

  • Creating if-then plans for obstacles beforehand allows you to consider all options and consequences rather than choosing something spur of the moment. Having a set response to specific obstacles allows you to detect critical situations easier as well as deal with them in a productive way.

What is the difference between a to-do and goal?

  • To-dos are relatively simple tasks and short-term. Goals are more general and long-term.

  • We recommend figuring out concrete, necessary to-dos that will help you achieve your goals. For example, some to-dos for the goal "get fit" could be "go to the gym twice a week" and "eat vegetables at lunch everyday"
